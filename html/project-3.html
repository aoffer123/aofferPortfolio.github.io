New Leaf

This application was created to help users develop and maintain good habits by providing a structured and motivating environment.

Project Overview

Project Duration

January - March 2022

The Problem

From January to March 2022, our project tackled the challenge of habit formation. While good habits lead to success, breaking bad habits is difficult due to immediate reinforcement. Most habit-tracking apps focus on completion and data visualization but lack mechanisms to help users consistently break bad habits and form good ones. Our solution, New Leaf, addresses this by encouraging users to replace bad habits with good ones through personalized reinforcement. The app allows users to create habits, categorize them, set goals, and define rewards, providing notifications and progress tracking to keep users motivated.

My role & Design Process

As the front-end developer and UI/UX designer, I was responsible for developing the user interface and managing the front-end aspects of New Leaf. I designed and implemented visual elements and interactions using the Material 3 component library, ensuring a seamless and responsive user experience. After finalizing paper wireframes, I developed high-fidelity prototypes in Figma, incorporating detailed visual designs and interactive components to simulate user interactions. This allowed for thorough testing and validation of the user experience before development. The prototypes were presented to stakeholders for feedback, ensuring the design met user needs. My role also involved collaborating with the backend team to integrate front-end functionality with server-side operations, creating a cohesive and functional app experience.
