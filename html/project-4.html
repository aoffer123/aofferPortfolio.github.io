Keystone by Irdeto mobile app

As an intern, I was brought in to redesign the fleet mobile application. My responsibilities included developing the user interface (UI) and managing all front-end aspects of the application. This involved creating a seamless and responsive user experience across different devices using the Material 3 component library. Additionally, I collaborated with the backend team to integrate front-end functionality with server-side operations, ensuring a cohesive and functional app experience.

Design Process

Low-Fidelity Prototype

The design process began with the creation of low-fidelity (low-fi) prototypes. I started by sketching out initial ideas and basic layouts on paper and then moved to digital wireframes. These low-fi prototypes were crucial in visualizing the overall structure and flow of the application without getting bogged down by details. They allowed for quick iterations and adjustments based on initial feedback from the team.

These early prototypes focused on functionality and user flow, helping to identify potential usability issues early on. By keeping the designs simple, we were able to quickly test different concepts and gather feedback, ensuring that the fundamental structure of the application met user needs and expectations before moving on to more detailed designs.

Design Process

Stakeholder Meetings & Iterations

Throughout the design process, regular stakeholder meetings were held to gather input and feedback. These meetings included team members from various departments, such as product management, development, sales, operations, and marketing. The goal was to ensure that the design aligned with the overall business objectives and addressed the needs of all stakeholders.

The feedback I recieved from these meetings was invaluable, leading to multiple iterations of the design. Each iteration incorporated stakeholder suggestions, progressively refining the interface. This collaborative approach ensured that the design evolved to meet both user needs and business goals, resulting in a more polished and effective product.

Design Process

High-fidelity Prototypes

After several iterations of low-fi prototypes and stakeholder feedback, the next step was to develop high-fidelity (hi-fi) prototypes. Utilizing the Material 3 component library, I focused on creating detailed and polished designs that closely resembled the final product. These hi-fi prototypes included precise visual elements, interactions, and animations to provide a realistic user experience.

The hi-fi prototypes aimed to streamline the flow of the app, ensuring a modern and seamless experience for users. By leveraging the Material 3 components, I was able to create a cohesive design language that was both aesthetically pleasing and functional. These prototypes went through many iterations, incorporating continuous feedback to refine the user interface and enhance the overall user experience before moving to development.
